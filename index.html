FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The recommended oil change frequency depends on the type of oil and vehicle. Conventional oil should be changed every 3,000 – 5,000 miles or every 6 months, while synthetic oil lasts between 7,500 – 10,000 miles or up to a year. Always refer to your vehicle’s manual for specific guidelines. Ignoring regular oil changes can lead to engine wear and reduced performance.

It’s important to check fluid levels regularly to keep your car running smoothly. Engine oil should be checked every 2-4 weeks, coolant once a month, and brake, power steering, and transmission fluids every few months. Windshield washer fluid should be refilled as needed. Low or dirty fluids can affect performance and safety.

You should change the oil if it turns dark or black, has a burnt smell, or if the oil warning light appears on your dashboard. Other signs include reduced engine performance or increased noise. Even if you don’t notice these signs, follow the recommended mileage intervals to prevent engine damage.

Brake pads usually last between 25,000 – 50,000 miles, but this varies based on driving habits. If you hear squeaking or grinding noises when braking, feel vibrations, or notice reduced stopping power, it’s time to replace them. Ignoring worn-out brake pads can lead to more expensive repairs and safety risks.

A car battery typically lasts 3-5 years, but extreme temperatures and frequent short trips can shorten its lifespan. Signs that you need a new battery include slow engine cranking, dim headlights, electrical issues, or corrosion on the battery terminals. If your battery is over three years old, have it tested regularly.

Tires should be rotated every 5,000 – 7,500 miles to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan. Regular rotation improves traction, handling, and fuel efficiency. Skipping tire rotations can lead to uneven tread wear, reducing safety and performance.
